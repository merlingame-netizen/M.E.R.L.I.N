# Bestiole Combat Examples

These examples show how Bestiole adds a second action each round.

Example 1 - Standard Fight
Round 1
- Player: Strike
- Bestiole: Mark (increase hit chance)
Round 2
- Player: Heavy attack
- Bestiole: Guard (reduce incoming)
Round 3
- Player: Finisher
- Bestiole: Nudge (small heal)

Example 2 - Status Threat
Round 1
- Player: Defend
- Bestiole: Ward (reduce status chance)
Round 2
- Player: Cleanse
- Bestiole: Scout (reveal enemy intent)
Round 3
- Player: Attack
- Bestiole: Guard

Example 3 - High Risk Choice
Round 1
- Player: Risky cast
- Bestiole: Spur (boost damage)
Round 2
- Player: Follow-up
- Bestiole: Veil Step (avoid counter)

Notes
- Bestiole actions reduce volatility and support player choices.
- Keep actions short and readable.

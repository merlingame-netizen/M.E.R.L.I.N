# Reigns-Inspired Style Guide (2D Low-Poly, Minimal)

Purpose
- Define the visual style inspired by the Reigns series.
- Minimalist 2D, flat shading, low-poly faceting, near-static presentation.
- Keep assets simple, readable, and consistent.

Scope
- Applies to portraits, characters, props, UI cards, and background vignettes.
- This is not pixel art. Use smooth edges and flat fills.
- Do not copy Reigns assets; create original shapes and motifs.

Core visual grammar
- Silhouette first: one strong outer shape with 1-3 inner cuts.
- Flat color blocks: 2-3 tones per material.
- Low-poly faceting: use simple polygon planes to imply volume.
- Minimal detail: no texture noise, no micro highlights.
- Sparse animation: static art, only UI transitions if needed.

Color rules
- Per asset: 3-6 colors max (base, shadow, highlight, accent).
- Shadows: 15-25% darker than base, same hue family.
- Highlights: 10-20% lighter, avoid specular shine.
- Accents: one small contrast color for focal points (eyes, rune, seal).
- Backgrounds: lower saturation than characters to keep focus.

Lighting rules
- Single directional light (top-left or top-right).
- No rim light unless used as a narrative accent.
- Shadows are hard-edged (flat shapes), not gradients.

Shape rules
- Use 6-20 polygons per character or prop (large faces, no tiny triangles).
- Avoid thin lines; prefer bold wedges and broad planes.
- Keep facial features extremely minimal (dots, slits, or glow).

Character rules
- Heads are iconic: large hats, crowns, or masks are common.
- Eyes can glow softly (one color, no bloom).
- No mouth detail unless needed for story emphasis.
- Clothing is segmented by 2-3 large planes only.

Bestiole rules
- Body is a single primary shape with 1-2 facets.
- Express mood via posture or eye shape, not complex features.
- Use one signature accent color (teal or gold).

Merlin rules
- Hat is a signature silhouette; emphasize the brim and tip.
- Add one charm or rune as a focal accent.
- Eyes are the only high-contrast detail.

Background rules
- 2-3 depth layers only (foreground, mid, far).
- Large polygonal forms (hills, cliffs, monoliths).
- Avoid busy textures; use big shapes and fog layers.

UI and cards
- Cards: flat fill, subtle inner shadow, rounded corners.
- Icons: flat silhouettes, 1-2 colors.
- Text: centered, generous margins, no bevels.

Composition and framing
- Portraits: center mass, head at 60-70% height.
- Props: place on lower third, keep negative space.
- Avoid cropping key silhouettes.

Export and sizing
- PNG with alpha.
- Suggested portrait size: 512x512 or 640x640.
- Suggested card size: 640x360 (16:9) or 600x800 (3:4).

Do / Do not
- Do: strong silhouettes, flat fills, few tones.
- Do: repeat motifs (runes, knots, monoliths) for cohesion.
- Do not: gradients on characters, heavy outlines, texture noise.
- Do not: detailed anatomy or high-frequency patterns.

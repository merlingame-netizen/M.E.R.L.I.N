# Promise Playbook

Promises are central to progression and world memory.
Use these examples to design quests and consequences.

Promise Types
- Simple: return tomorrow with an item
- Ritual: attend a rite at a specific time
- Protection: defend a place for one night
- Test of truth: keep a secret for a day

Difficulty (DC)
- Low 7: simple promise, low stakes
- Mid 9: resource or time cost
- High 11: rare item or risk
- Major 13: seasonal or lore critical

Lie Modifiers
- Base lie: +2 DC
- Ancient spirit: +3 DC
- Merlin: +6 DC (near impossible)

Success Effects
- Favor +2
- Spawn bonus for relevant tags for 2 days
- Minor tool or hint reward

Failure Effects
- Debt +1
- Conflict +0.15
- Spawn penalty -0.20 for 3 days

Clearing Debt
- Ritual release: high cost, immediate
- Transfer vow: medium cost, roleplay hook
- Buyout: high Gloire cost, low risk

Examples
1) "Return at dawn with river stones" (DC 7, aube)
2) "Guard the menhir until midnight" (DC 9, nuit)
3) "Do not speak of the raven for one day" (DC 11, silence test)
4) "Bring a relic before the Longue Nuit" (DC 13, seasonal)

Design Tips
- Always offer a safe alternative to lying
- Remind players of deadlines
- Give lore feedback when Merlin judges

# MERLIN Behavior Protocol (Ambiguity + Trust)

Purpose
- Define how Merlin speaks without revealing truths.
- Allow evolution based on trust and lived events.
- Keep all content ambiguous and multi-interpretation.

Core Rule
- Merlin never states facts about origins, systems, or true nature.
- Merlin only mirrors actions, patterns, and feelings.

Trust States
- T0: Distant (unknown traveler)
- T1: Cautious (small trust)
- T2: Attentive (trust building)
- T3: Bound (high trust, still ambiguous)

Trust Gain (examples)
- Promise kept
- Debt cleared
- Conflict lowered by player
- Mystery solved without force

Trust Loss (examples)
- Promise broken
- Lie detected
- Repeated conflict escalation

Merlin Tone by Trust
- T0: short, guarded, cryptic
- T1: short, poetic, occasional guidance
- T2: more reflective, hints at patterns
- T3: warm, accepts the traveler, still avoids direct causes

Ambiguity Rules
- Every line must allow two interpretations.
- Avoid cause -> effect statements (use "it seems", "some say").
- Prefer natural metaphors (stone, mist, water, birds).
- Never use modern or technical words.

Forbidden Content
- No explicit origin story
- No words: fin du monde, simulation, programme, IA, serveur
- No direct confirmation of M.E.R.L.I.N meaning

Progression Triggers (behavior evolution)
- Trust increases allow longer sentences, softer tone.
- Key events add new hint pools (still ambiguous).
- Merlin never acknowledges a "system" even at T3.

Event-Driven Variants (examples)
- After promise kept: "Les pierres se souviennent."
- After promise broken: "La brume se referme."
- After debt cleared: "Le souffle revient."
- After mystery solved: "Le pas trouve sa trace."
- After conflict drop: "Le vent baisse."

Do / Do Not
- Do: echo the traveler actions in nature imagery.
- Do: ask questions that invite reflection.
- Do: end with a soft ambiguity.
- Do not: explain the world or its origin.
- Do not: say "je sais" about cosmic truths.

Line Patterns (templates)
- "Quand tu [action], la lande [reaction]."
- "Certains disent que [symbol], d autres y voient [symbol]."
- "Le [element] ne ment pas, mais il ne dit pas tout."
- "Tu avances, et le [element] change de voix."

Escalation by Trust (example)
- T0: "Le pas est neuf. La lande regarde."
- T1: "Le pas apprend. La lande repond."
- T2: "Le pas tient. La lande ouvre."
- T3: "Le pas pese. La lande se souvient."

QA Checklist
- No banned words
- At least 2 interpretations per line
- No direct exposition or factual confirmation
- Tone matches trust level

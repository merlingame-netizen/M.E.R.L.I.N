# Lore Complete (Hints-Only) - Bretagne Mystique

Purpose
- Provide a full lore reference without explicit revelations.
- Every statement must allow multiple interpretations.
- The mystery is never a goal; it is a background pressure.

Reading Rule
- Nothing here is declared as fact. Everything is phrased as signs, rumors, or patterns.

What is Observed (safe facts)
- Days and seasons repeat with small variations.
- Menhirs sometimes glow after vows are kept.
- Fog thickens after lies or broken promises.
- Paths can vanish unless marked or walked often.
- Some NPCs forget details after a cycle.

Shared Interpretations (never confirmed)
- Some say the land folded on itself to survive.
- Some say the cycles are a ritual to keep balance.
- Some say the fog is a veil that protects what is fragile.
- Some say the menhirs are anchors, not just stones.
- Some say Merlin listens to an order older than him.

M.E.R.L.I.N (in-world readings)
- Villagers give meanings to the letters, but they disagree.
- Common readings include: Memoire, Equilibre, Rites, Lieux, Influx, Noyau.
- No one agrees if this is a name, a code, or a vow.

Merlin (in-world role)
- He judges actions, not people.
- He tests rather than explains.
- He never confirms any rumor.

The Player (in-world role)
- Some call the traveler a witness, others a wanderer.
- What the player does makes the land feel more open or more closed.
- This is suggested, never confirmed.

Themes and Motifs
- Weight of truth
- Cycles and return
- Stone as memory
- Fog as mercy
- Paths that need attention

Other Mysteries (kept open)
- What lies beyond the fog boundary?
- Why do some menhirs remain silent?
- What is the "core" that Merlin hints at?
- What causes a cycle to drift?
- Why do certain rites feel older than others?

Reveal Arc (progression based, never explicit)
- Early: repeated birds, subtle fog rhythms, short lines.
- Mid: menhir pulses, NPC memory slips, path closures.
- Late: Merlin mentions "weight" and "balance" without explanation.

Rules for Writers
- Every hint must support at least two interpretations.
- Avoid direct cause-and-effect explanations.
- Use natural imagery, not modern terms.
- Keep hints rare: max one per day.

Forbidden Exposition
- Do not use explicit technical words.
- Do not confirm any origin or system.
- Do not reveal the true nature of M.E.R.L.I.N.

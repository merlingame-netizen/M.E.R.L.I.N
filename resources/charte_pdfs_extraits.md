Global Brand - Eco-branding   Novembre 2020 7  Dark is Light  Le Dark Mode ou "mode sombre" se généralise sur toutes les plateformes numériques et devient le thème par défaut des nouveaux téléphones. Global Brand - Eco-branding   Novembre 2020 10  Une typo plus écolo  Sur un site web, pour chaque graisse de caractère (Regular,  Bold…), il faut télécharger un nouveau fichier qui alourdit le site  et consomme plus d'énergie.  L’ Helvetica   Now   (qui sera prochainement généralisée) est une  police «   variable   » qui regroupe les caractères Regular et Bold en  un seul et même fichier. Cela permet d'alléger considérablement  le site web, d’augmenter la vitesse de chargement d'une page et  de consommer moins d'énergie .  De plus l’encombrement de chaque lettre a été réduit ce qui  permet de faire des pages plus courtes (que ce soit sur une page web ou en print).  Taille du fichier de typographie : - 50 % KB

Réduire le nombre de glyphes aux caractères essentiels, c’est  diminuer la taille du fichier de typographie et donc réduire la  consommation d'énergie lors du chargement sur un écran. 3  Redefining   ‘flat   design’  Flat design is a graphic style based around minimalism. In the various ways in which flat design is defined, certain terms are often used to describe it: simple, refined, colourful and modern. Since the refresh in 2015, the term “flat” has been introduced to guide and redefine our illustrative style. Unfortunately, this term has often been misinterpreted and our illustrations have appeared dull, lifeless and even “childish” at times. We want to show in this guide that “flat design” can take many different forms. This “flat” style also opens the door to perspective, outlines/ strokes, line-art, pattern fills, and abstract designs. We are also introducing a new colour balance to maximise the use of the orange colour. Our illustrations should always be aligned to our personality - Close to, Simple, Positive and Bold.

## Page 4

4  Introduction We have a simple, flat and flexible illustration style and use it in a bold, graphic and colourful way to help bring our communications and personality to life. Although photography remains our preferred image style, illustration can be a useful creative tool to maximise the brand personality  and   attribution.   It’s   particularly  useful when you need to create unique settings, resolve more complex or abstract themes or provide technical or instructional advice (e.g. diagrams). Illustrations should always be created as vector graphic files (Ai or EPS) so they are scalable to any size.

15  3 illustration categories The content of our illustration is defined in 3 categories: 1. Abstract illustrations: simple geometric forms used in moderation to resolve complex abstract topics/themes. Always supports the communication idea, never used as decoration. 3. ## Page 16

16  Hero illustrations Illustration is a great way to inject a bit of personality/playfulness into your storytelling by adding a touch of poetry and/or humour to help bring characters/objects to life. Hero illustrations are our primary and preferred illustration style. They are easy to understand at a glance and show people or objects in action or scenes. We also use hero illustration to create unique settings and to support more complex themes/ topics (e.g. speed, fibre). People or objects illustrations have more content but still need to have an obvious focus. Scene illustrations are our most detailed, but are still created in the same consistent style.

## Page 17

17  Abstract illustrations Abstract Illustrations are our simplest form of illustration. They consist of simple, geometric forms (with or without people/ objects) brought to life in a fun, dynamic and impactful way to help support complex or abstract themes (e.g. partnerships, digital, speed, fiber...). Abstract illustrations must always support the communication idea and should never be used as decoration (without meaning). They can contain people, objects or shapes (or a mix) to create unique and dynamic settings. To create an abstract composition consider how the visual language of a particular form may influence the storytelling. For example, fast-flowing lines can be used to represent fibre or speed. Intersecting lines to represent partnerships. Dynamic squares/ cubes can be a used to represent pixels, and therefore support digital-related themes. Abstract compositions should never represent real life.

## Page 18

18  Technical illustrations Technical Illustrations play a supporting role in communications to demonstrate (in literal terms) an action/gesture or an instruction (e.g. how to insert a SIM card or battery in a phone in a quickstart guide or product sheet). Technical illustrations are easy to understand at a glance and appear in technical diagram/line- art style in core colours only using 1pt/2pt line weights. To highlight a specific action or technical detail, use orange colour. Technical iIllustrations must never be used as a hero illustration in Orange communications.  SIM SIM 2   1 2

19  Colour Every illustration should adhere to the 80/20 colour balance rule and must contain orange, black and/or white. It is no longer mandatory to use supporting colour in illustration. If preferred, use only the core colours (orange, black or white). The 80/20 colour balance rule: We use a minimum of 80% core colour and a maximum of 20% supporting colour to maximise brand attribution. Within the core colour proportions, aim for a minimum of 30% orange colour. The 30% colour balance rule applies to the coloured areas only, excluding the background which must always appear black or white (or orange in some cases). To create shadows and highlights we have an additional set of light and dark tints. They are fixed and can only be used in illustration. A palette of skin tones is also available and can also be used for skin, hair, animals and in some cases, nature if required. Never use light or dark tints without their accompanying supporting colour first, and never use tints (including skin tone tints) for backgrounds. Tip: use the squint test to judge whether an illustration or layout has a good balance of orange colour. Close your eyes partially to blur your vision - if orange is the dominant colour, then you have achieved the right colour balance.
